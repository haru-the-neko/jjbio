COMING OF AGE

Childhood Years

          I was born on November 21, 2004, in a small town in Quezon.1 My home was consisted of my dad, mom, and my younger siblings.2 I also have three older half sisters that lives in my grandparents house.3 We were pretty closed to each other, I remember my dad was stricter than my mom, but they were always supportive of whatever we did.4 I grew up in a quiet rural neighborhood, it felt safe, and everyone knew each other.5 We lived in a two-story house with a big front yard and garage, perfect for playing with my siblings.6 I remember we used to dig for worms on our yard and play with it.7 I didn’t have a lot of friends back then since I rarely went outside.8 I usually just hang with my siblings and my sister’s friends which I also considered my sisters.9 We would watch movies and series that was on CD’s back then, spent hours playing console games, and we also play pretend adventures in the woods behind our grandparents’ house, where we’d imagine we were explorers on some grand quests.10 And those are just few of my fav things to do whenever we hung out.11 I attended Jose Rizal Elementary, a small school that felt more like a community than an institution.12 The teachers knew everyone by name, and I always felt supported there.13 My favorite subject was art because I liked to draw, and just because I also hated writing a lot.14 I did well overall in school, I did excel in math at that time because my dad made me memorize all the multiplication table from 1 to 10.15 He also taught me the fingers trick, if I couldn’t remember them and had a lot of addition and subtraction.16 My parents taught me the value of kindness, always reminding me that being a good person mattered more than being the best at anything.17 We also had a pet Labrador named Erap, He was our constant companion, and also guards our house.18 My grandparents also played a huge role in my childhood. I spent summers at their house, where they would tell me a lot of stories and also make meals for me.19 My family also took trips every vacation, my fav one was when we went to Manila Ocean Park.20 It was my first time seeing a penguin.21 I still have a lot of things to tell, but that pretty sums up my childhood. Overall, my childhood was filled with love, adventure, and lessons that have stayed with me to this day.22

Teenage Years

          I became a teenager when I turned 13, and one of the major changes was starting high school.1 It felt like a whole new world compared to middle school.2 I went to Sariaya Institute because that’s where my parents finished high school.3 It was much bigger than my previous school.4 It was diverse, with a mix of students from a different backgrounds, and I like the feeling of being part of something bigger.5 I came to that school not knowing anyone and without my friends from middle school.6 But I did make some new friends during my teenage year.7 I became close with people who shared similar humor, interest in music, sports and also foods, and we’d hangout every lunch break and after school, especially on weekends.8 I also joined the art’s club where we’re the ones who’d make decorations every time that there is an event.9 My relationship with my parents was generally good, though we had our share of disagreements, especially about curfew and freedom. But, I know that deep down, they were just worried and were always supportive of my whatever I’m doing.10 During my teenage years, I play a lot of video games and was also really into music.11 I spent a lot time listening to different genres, my favorite artist and bands at that time was Frank Ocean, Steve lacy, Djo, and Men I trust.12 Most of my free time was spent hanging out with my friends, playing video games, or going to their house to play ball.13 Weekends we’re usually going out somewhere.14 I had a hard time balancing academics and social life that time.15 I struggled a bit with time management and sometimes found it very hard to stay on top of everything.16 I developed a long lasting friendship during high school.17 Some of those people are still my close friends today, and we’ve supported each other through the good and bad times.18 High school is also when I met my girlfriend, who is still my girlfriend to this day.19 There were many places we would go to hang out after school but, there was this street just beside our school that we called our “tambayan” its was like our meeting place where we would talk about our plans or decide where we were going.20 We would usually go to the beach before sunset or at night just to hang out.21 I remember we would blast music, chase and catch some crabs, get drunk, swim, or just talk about anything.22 Those were the good times in my life.23 I still have a lot of things to tell, but that pretty sums up my teenage years and looking back at it, it was a year were a time of growth, self-discovery, and learning how to navigate the ups and downs of life, and they play a huge role in shaping who I am today.24
